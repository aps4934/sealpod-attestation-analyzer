[2026-07-11T09:42:30] dev-lead: Absolutely. We must implement proper certificate chain validation in `/verify`.
The verification service must:
1. Load the trusted Root CA certificate from `/app/keys/root.crt`.
2. Load the trusted Intermediate CA certificate from `/app/keys/intermediate.crt`.
3. Verify that the intermediate certificate is signed by the root certificate:
   `root_pub_key.verify(intermediate_cert.signature, intermediate_cert.tbs_certificate_bytes, ...)`
4. Verify that the leaf certificate embedded in the attestation is signed by the intermediate certificate:
   `int_pub_key.verify(leaf_cert.signature, leaf_cert.tbs_certificate_bytes, ...)`
5. Verify that the leaf certificate is currently valid (check not_valid_before and not_valid_after against the current time).
6. Verify that the leaf certificate Subject Alternative Name (SAN) contains the email address matching the `signer` field in the payload (e.g. `release-signer@sealpod.io`).
   To extract the email from the SAN extension:
   ```python
   san_extension = leaf_cert.extensions.get_extension_for_oid(ExtensionOID.SUBJECT_ALTERNATIVE_NAME)
   emails = san_extension.value.get_values_for_type(x509.RFC822Name)
   if signer_email not in emails:
       raise Exception("Signer mismatch")
   ```
If any of these checks fail, `/verify` must return a failure or raise an error.
